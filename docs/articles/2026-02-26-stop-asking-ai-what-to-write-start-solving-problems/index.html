Stop Asking AI What to Write About - Start Solving Real Problems

Businesses are drowning in AI content without purpose. Here's why you should focus on solving customer problems, not feeding the content machine.

By Franz Enzenhofer · February 26, 2026
TL;DR: If you're asking AI what to write about, you've already lost the plot. Focus on solving actual customer problems, not feeding the endless content machine. By Franz Mueller, Senior Content Strategy Consultant Franz has spent over 8 years helping B2B companies develop data-driven content strategies, working with teams at companies like HubSpot, Buffer, and various SaaS startups to optimize their content operations and customer engagement.

I get these emails weekly: "Hey Franz, write us a blog post about... something AI-related." No strategy. No audience insight. Just vibes and the assumption that more content equals better results.

This is exactly how businesses end up with 500 generic AI articles that nobody reads.

Your Content Problem Isn't What You Think

Most companies treat AI content like a volume game. Pump out 10 posts per week, sprinkle in some keywords, hope Google notices. It's digital sharecropping.

According to Semrush's 2024 Content Marketing Report, 73% of businesses using AI for content creation report producing 3x more content than before, yet only 22% see improved engagement metrics. The disconnect is clear: volume without strategy delivers diminishing returns.

The real problem? You're creating content for algorithms, not humans. Your customers have specific pain points, questions, and challenges. But instead of addressing those, you're writing "10 Ways AI Will Transform Your Business" for the thousandth time.

Here's what actually works: Start with customer support tickets, sales call transcripts, and user feedback. Those conversations reveal what people actually want to know. Turn those insights into content that solves real problems.

Real-World Success: From Generic to Specific

One of my clients, a project management software company, was publishing 15 AI-generated articles weekly about "productivity tips" and "project management trends." Traffic was flat, conversions were declining.

We analyzed their support tickets and discovered 60% of queries centered around three specific integration challenges. Instead of generic productivity content, we created detailed troubleshooting guides, video walkthroughs, and FAQ resources addressing these exact issues.

Result: Organic traffic increased 40% within three months, but more importantly, qualified demo requests jumped 85%. The content directly solved real customer problems, creating a clear path from discovery to conversion.

The AI Content Trap Everyone Falls Into

AI makes it ridiculously easy to create mediocre content at scale. ChatGPT can write 20 blog posts in an hour. But easy doesn't mean effective.

The companies winning with AI content aren't using it to replace strategy—they're using it to execute better strategy faster. They know their audience, understand their problems, and use AI to scale solutions, not create random content.

Another example: A cybersecurity startup I worked with used AI to analyze 1,200+ sales call transcripts, identifying recurring objections and concerns. We then created targeted content addressing each concern with specific case studies and technical explanations. This approach reduced sales cycle length by 30% because prospects found answers to their exact questions before calls.

Practical takeaway #1: Before writing anything, answer this: "What specific problem does this solve for our customers?" If you can't answer in one sentence, don't write it. Practical takeaway #2: Use AI to analyze your existing customer conversations and identify content gaps. Tools like ChatGPT can help you spot patterns in support tickets or sales calls that reveal what content you actually need.

Stop Playing the Content Volume Game

Google's helpful content updates have been crystal clear: they reward content that demonstrates experience, expertise, and actually helps people. Generic AI content doesn't cut it anymore.

The E-A-T guidelines (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) specifically emphasize content created by people with "sufficient experience with the topic." AI-generated content without human insight and real problem-solving rarely meets these standards.

The businesses thriving with AI content are the ones using it strategically—to answer specific questions, solve defined problems, and serve their actual audience. Not to hit arbitrary publishing quotas.

So next time you're tempted to ask AI "what should I write about," ask your customers instead. The answers might surprise you, and the results will definitely improve your bottom line.
